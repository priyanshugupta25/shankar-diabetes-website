Why is regular diabetes monitoring important?

Regular monitoring helps maintain healthy blood sugar levels, prevents complications, and supports early detection of issues related to the heart, kidneys, eyes, nerves, and liver.

What is FibroScan and how does it help diabetic patients?

FibroScan is a non-invasive liver assessment technology used to detect liver stiffness and fatty liver disease, conditions commonly associated with diabetes and metabolic disorders.

Why are Foot Check-ups necessary for diabetic patients?

Diabetes can reduce blood circulation and nerve sensitivity in the feet. Regular foot examinations help prevent ulcers, infections, and serious diabetic foot complications.

What is a Fundus Eye Check-up?

A Fundus Check-up is a retinal examination that helps detect diabetic retinopathy and other eye-related complications at an early stage, helping protect long-term vision.

What is the purpose of the Lunar DXA Scanner?

The Lunar DXA Scanner helps measure bone density and detect osteoporosis early, which is important for diabetic patients who may be at increased risk of bone-related issues.

How often should I visit the clinic for diabetes management?

The frequency of visits depends on your condition and treatment plan. Most diabetic patients are advised to schedule regular follow-ups every 3–6 months for effective monitoring and care.
